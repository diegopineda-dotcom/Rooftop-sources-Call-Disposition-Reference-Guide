Call Disposition Reference Guide

Disposition Description Examples
NO ANSWER / VOICEMAIL The call was not answered by a person or went directly to a voicemail service. - Phone rings multiple times with no pickup.
- "Hi, you've reached..." message plays.
HANG UP BEFORE PITCH The contact answers but hangs up the phone before the agent can deliver the full introduction or value proposition. - Agent: "Hi, my name is..." *click*
- Contact answers, hears a pause, and hangs up.
WRONG NUMBER The person who answered is not the intended contact or homeowner on the lead list. - "John Smith doesn't live here anymore."
- "This is a business line."
BAD LEAD The lead is invalid for reasons other than a wrong number. They do not meet the core qualifying criteria for the campaign. - "I live in an apartment / I'm a renter."
- "My house has a metal/tile roof, not shingles."
- "I work for a roofing company."
DNC (Do Not Call) The contact explicitly requests to be removed from the calling list and not be contacted again. - "Please put me on your do not call list."
- "Stop calling this number."
UNDER AGED ROOF The lead's roof is too new to qualify for the inspection service based on campaign requirements. - "We just had our roof replaced last year."
- "My roof is only 3 years old."
NOT INTERESTED The contact has heard the agent's pitch but declines the offer. - "Thanks, but I'm not interested at this time."
- "I'm fine, thanks!."
APPOINTMENT SET / SEND EMAIL -The lead is qualified, interested, and has successfully scheduled an appointment for the next day.
-An email with all the details will be send to the mananger. 		- "Yes, tomorrow at 2 PM works perfectly for me."
- All scheduling details confirmed on the call.
FOLLOW-UP -The homeowner is interested but the appointment is either not confirmed yet or has been scheduled for a future date (not the next day). -The agent must follow up and confirm or reconfirm the appointment one day before to mark it as a "APPOINTMENT SET"
